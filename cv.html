Philosophy, professional interests

I am a firm believer and advocate of agile techniques in every aspect of the life cycle of a software product. That involves handling requirements (e.g less is more), project management (e.g short iterations), development (BDD and pair programming) and involving the client.

I like to think of myself and striving to be a software craftsman and consider it very important (and joyful!) to constantly hone my existing skills and acquire new ones. (e.g learning a new programming language every year). A true craftsman must be pride of his craft and make excellent products.

Philosophy, professional interests

I embrace the idea of "be a generalist, be a specialist" and am consequently looking to attain at least a basic understanding but preferably an adequate skill-set of programming-related domains. Notably, I am really attracted to web design and user interface design and I think "the web" has a lot to catch up on in this latter.

I enjoy going to conferences and talking to similar-minded people about the problems, challenges and joys that we face every day as developers. Or about anything else, in fact. It became so easy to communicate with anyone, anywhere, that we sometimes tend to forget nothing can replace in-person communication.
